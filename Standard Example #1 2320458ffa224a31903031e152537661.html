Standard example #1

  • The standard codes and procedures were developed by NIST to have different softwares running similar results on some of the fundamental phenomenon.
  • There are 5 standard problems that are suggested by the NIST standard. Here we consider a version of the Standard problem 1 as an example to make yourself familiar with the simulations of mumax3.

The initial state is an equilibrium state is obtained after applying and slowly reducing a saturating field magnetic field along the [1,1,1] direction to zero. Fields of sufficient magnitude are applied to reverse the magnetization of the rectangle. The time evolution of the magnetization as the system moves towards equilibrium in the new fields are examined.

STEP 2: Convert descriptions into code

  1. Define the material structure and properties.
  1. “relax()” function bring the system to equilibrium or low energy state.
  1. Save Initial m.
  1. Define the initial m state with uniform domains in x-direction.
  1. Autosave m state every 10−11s10^{-11} s10−11s﻿, ”tableautosave” is to log the data to table.tsv file.
  1. Applying the external magnetic field with B_ext, in milli Tesla (mT).
  1. “run()” method initializes and run the solver to find numerical solutions to the LLG Partial differential equation. The simulation time is defined with 1ns parameter to the function.

//define geometry
SetGridsize(128, 32, 1)
SetCellsize(500e-9/128, 125e-9/32, 3e-9)

//define materials
Msat = 800e3
Aex= 13e-12
alpha = 0.02

//define magnetization state
m = uniform(1, .1, 0)
relax()
save(m) // relaxed state‐equilibrium state

//define output
autosave(m, 200e-12)
tableautosave(10e-12)

//applied magnetic field
B_ext = vector(-24.6E-3, 4.3E-3, 0)

//run the simulation for 1ns
run(1e-9)

  1. Output data is plotted for magnetization vs time graph. This shows the magnetization in the x-direction is switching.
