Standard Example

The initial state is an equilibrium state such as is obtained after applying and slowly reducing a saturating field along the [1,1,1] direction to zero. Fields of magnitude sufficient to reverse the magnetization of the rectangle are applied to this initial state and the time evolution of the magnetization as the system moves towards equilibrium in the new fields are examined.

STEP 1: collect the related parameters
  • Geometry
    • The film with the thickness of 3 nm, the length of 500 nm,  and the width of 125 nm will be used.
  • Material parameters
    • A = 1.3×10‐11 J/m (1.3e‐6 erg/cm), Ms = 8.0×105 A/m (800 emu/cc),  K = 0.0
  • The dynamics
    • Landau‐Lifshitz equation
    • Gilbert equation

  • Applied Field
    • µ0Hx = ‐24.6 mT, µ0Hy = 4.3 mT, µ0Hz = 0.0 mT
    • The field will be applied instantaneously at t=0 to the equilibrium state.

STEP 2: Convert descriptions into codes
  • Geometry
    • The film with the thickness of 3 nm, the length of 500 nm, and the width of 125 nm will be used.
  • Material parameters
    • A = 1.3×10‐11 J/m (1.3e‐6 erg/cm), Ms = 8.0×105 A/m (800 emu/cc), K = 0.0
  • Define the initial m state, and save m state every 10-11 s.
  • Applied Field
    • µ0Hx = ‐24.6 mT, µ0Hy = 4.3 mT, µ0Hz = 0.0 mT
//define geometry
SetGridsize(128, 32, 1)
SetCellsize(500e-9/128, 125e-9/32, 3e-9)

//define materials
Msat = 800e3
Aex= 13e-12
alpha = 0.02

//define magnetization state
m = uniform(1, .1, 0)
relax()
save(m) // relaxed state‐equilibrium state

//define output
autosave(m, 200e-12)
tableautosave(10e-12)

//applied magnetic field
B_ext = vector(-24.6E-3, 4.3E-3, 0)

//run the simulation for 1ns
run(1e-9)
