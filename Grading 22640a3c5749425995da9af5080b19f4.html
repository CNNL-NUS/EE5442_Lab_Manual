Grading

The grading conditions and their breakup are provided here for reference. The grading across the four Labs will have equal weightage.

There is NO Final Examination in this iteration of the course.

Team/Group Contribution

The Lab assignment / tasks have to be done in groups (decided by the students previously).

  1. One report per group should be submitted to Canvas-NUS. If more than one report is submitted by the other team members, the report shall incur penalty.
  1. One of the account from the group should have the final scripts and results in a sub folder “Milestone_0X” under “imem0XX”. This should be mentioned in the report.

(Simulations can be run on personal computers (PC) or other online computing nodes, but the final script and results should be submitted on the server).

  1. Working on the Milestones is a group effort. The whole group/team will be graded equally based on their performance and the reports.
  1. Submissions should be done before the deadline, any submission with 48hrs after the deadline will incur 50% penalty in the score. Submission after the 48hrs limit, will not be graded.
  1. Student changing the group in midway (without valid reason and without informing the GAs & professor) will lose the lab contribution from their final grading.
  1. The score weightage for the report and its contents vary based on the lab. Please visit the respective Lab session landing page for details.

Critical Conditions

If these conditions are not met the grading will not be done for the particular Team.

  1. Submission of AUP form (Individual).
  1. Acknowledgement Form for each Lab Report.
  1. Report submission after 48hrs after the deadline (Weekends Saturday 2359hrs) / No report.
  1. Submission of the scripts and results in the “imem0XX” folder, failing which will incur penalty for the lab session.
  1. Attendance to the Lab Sessions.
  1. No changing or swapping between the groups once finalized.

For any appeals or request please email the GAs.

The decision will be taken on case by case basis, if there is a valid reason from the student. For medical reasons, proofs or certificates will be required, depending on the case.

Students are expected to follow a Honorary Code, where discussion with other students is allowed for the purpose of mutual growth and benefit. In scenarios where plagiarism is found, it shall be responded in highest level, leading to even failing of the course.
